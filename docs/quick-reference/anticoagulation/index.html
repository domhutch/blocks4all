Anticoagulation & Blocks

Timing of nerve blocks in anticoagulated patients, based on ESRA 2022 guidelines and NUTH local guidance.

:::warning Key Principle The classification of a block as deep or superficial determines whether anticoagulation timing rules apply. Superficial blocks may generally be performed without cessation of anticoagulants. Deep blocks require consideration of timing from last dose. :::

:::info Superficial blocks Superficial nerve blocks may all be performed without cessation of anticoagulant or antithrombotic drugs, provided any relevant level (INR, anti-Xa) is within or below the therapeutic range, and there is no clinical suspicion of overdose or drug accumulation. :::

Key points​

  • The ESRA guidelines separate blocks into deep and superficial according to risk of provoking non-compressible or catastrophic bleeding
  • For deep blocks in anticoagulated patients: consider timing carefully, document rationale, and discuss with supervising consultant if performed by a trainee
  • Performing a block that contravenes ESRA timing (e.g. for acute trauma) must be a documented risk-benefit decision in the patient's notes and consent form
  • Therapeutic LMWH should not be given when a nerve or epidural catheter is in situ
  • Trainees performing blocks on anticoagulated patients must have appropriate supervision
